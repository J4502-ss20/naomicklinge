Naomi Klinge

Multimedia Reporter | Digital | International

As a student at the University of Missouri studying International Journalism and Political Science, I have had the chance to become an experienced reporter. I currently report for Missouri Business Alert, a statewide online publication. I have also worked as a reporter at the Columbia Missourian, a city newspaper, and as a news and digital producer at KOMU 8 News, an NBC affiliate.

Last year I spent 7 months abroad, a huge step in my goal to one day work internationally. I first spent four months as a communications intern at International Crisis Group, a conflict resolution NGO in Brussels, which introduced me to the conflict world, something I've always been incredibly interested in. I then moved to Delhi for the summer to work as a reporting intern at the Indian Express, a prominent english-language newspaper that allowed me to begin reporting on human rights and immigration, a topic I plan to feature heavily in my work moving forward. I also produced a project about apartheid during a summer in South Africa, and I lived with a host family in the Dominican Republic during a summer in high school.

Now I want to continue reporting on groups who don't always have the chance to speak for themselves and dedicate my time to understanding how cultures and communities intersect with socio-political movements.

At street art festival, participants bring varied palette of skills

As my first pitch at Missouri Business Alert, I had no idea how to talk about business, so I went with what I know: art! I thought my editor would immediately reject the idea, but we sat and figured out how to make it relevant for the publication's target audience, showing how artists are entrepreneurs.

For thousands along the Yamuna, no option but to wait for the water to rise

I had the chance to work on several amazing stories in Delhi, but this was the first I was lead reporter on. Because of language barriers, I was mostly assisting and shadowing other journalists, but for this piece I did the research and pitched the story, so I took charge as another reporter translated for me.

Black Theater: From repression to integration

My first major reporting project was in Cape Town, South Africa, where I traveled with a group from my university. As someone who has been involved with theater my whole life, I decided to build on that knowledge and do a project about the history of black theater in the country.
